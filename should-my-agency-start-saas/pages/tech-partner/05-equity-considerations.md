# Don't be scared to give equity

<v-clicks>

- If you're thinking "I don't want to give away half of my new tech product's equity to a partner, we already have 2 co-founders from our agency. The split would be tiny."
- You don't need to give 50% to the tech partner. With your agency's revenue, you should be able to provide a competitive comp package by mixing salary with equity

</v-clicks>

---

# The Math

<v-clicks>

- Let's say there's you and two more agency co-founders. That's 4 people → 25% each
- With revenue of $2M/yr, you'll be looking at a valuation of $10-14M (already a 5-7x of what an agency doing $2M/yr is worth). That's $2.5-3.5M each
- In a scenario where you didn't have the tech partner, you'd each have 33%, $3.3-4.62M. So in essence the tech co-founder took $0.8-1.1M from you

</v-clicks>

---

# The Value

<v-clicks>

- In my eyes as someone that's studied hundreds of SaaS stories, it's so rare that I find one where they didn't have a tech co-founder
- And that's not even considering the survivorship bias
- I personally would give up that money in exchange to actually get to that point *and* to get there faster

</v-clicks>
