Who am I to tell you how to go from Agency to SaaS?

We ran our AI SaaS development and consulting business for over a year, serving nine-to-five professionals trying to start startups along with building our own projects.

As a business owner, you see already the obvious problems: our clients lacked the essential traits to succeed as SaaS founders. Things like personality traits that business owners need (Type-A personalities) and any business acumen in sales or marketing.

The result was frustrating: we built great products in record time, following the playbooks of successful CTOs when their startups were at 0. But those products went nowhere once launched.

This pushed us to invest the next 6 months not taking in new any clients and investing a lot of time and money into what we lacked:
* understanding what makes SaaS businesses successful (since clearly we didn't; we chose 9-5 professionals as partners)
* mastering the growth side—sales, distribution, and go-to-market.

I came across the story of MailChimp and realized it was an agency-to-SaaS evolution. And that's when it clicked. Agency owners make the best SaaS founders.

We discovered David Hart's book and dove in, which got us even more bullish on this Agency-to-SaaS niche. Being in both the AI and agency spaces, we also knew the timing was urgent—AI and automation are making digital services increasingly redundant.

With this bullishness, I devoured podcasts, books, and stories of agencies transforming into SaaS companies.

And from endless days over weeks, I built the Agency-to-SaaS Playbook.
