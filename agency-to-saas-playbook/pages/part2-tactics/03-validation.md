# 1. Proximity to the Problem

<v-clicks>

- Only solve problems you or your close network face
- It's your strongest advantage
- Too much speculation to figure out distant problems

</v-clicks>

---

# 2. Time, Money, Headspace

<v-clicks>

- Magic wand test: "If this solution existed tomorrow..."
- Quantify the value
- Hard figures ground your idea in tangible value
- More savings = higher pricing potential
- Value = Time Saved + Money Saved/Made + Stress Reduced

</v-clicks>



---

# 3. Head on Fire Test

<v-clicks>

- Does the CEO lie awake at night worrying about this problem?
- Does your product unlock something that helps them solve it?
- Look for problems that cause genuine pain and urgency

</v-clicks>

---

# 4. Current Solutions Analysis

<v-clicks>

- How are they currently solving this problem?
- Hacky alternatives? Terrible competitors? Manual processes?
- Critical question: How will my SaaS stand out?
- "Cheaper" or "better UI"
- No competitors?
</v-clicks>

# Example: AI-powered Ad Campaign Generator for e-commerce

### Bottom-up market sizing

<v-clicks>

- Step 1: Calculate Price = Value you provide / 5
- Step 2: Calculate Revenue = Target Customers × Price × Market Penetration %

</v-clicks>

<v-clicks>

- Target Market: 50,000 companies in specific niche
- Pricing: $300/month ($3,600 annually)
- Max Revenue: $180M/yr
- Year 3 Revenue (1% market penetration): $1.8M/yr
- Year 5 Revenue (3% market penetration): $5.4M/yr
- Valuation: $21.6M - $43.2M

</v-clicks>

---

# Example: AI-powered Outbound Sales Assistant
### TAM validation

<v-clicks>

- Step 1: Identify total companies that need your solution
- Step 2: Research their current annual spending on related tools
- Step 3: Estimate what percentage of their budget your solution could capture

</v-clicks>

<v-clicks>

- Total Companies: 1,000,000
- Average Annual Ad Spend: $500,000
- Your Solution's Share: 2% of marketing budget ($10,000/yr of value)
- Total TAM: $10B/yr
- 0.1% market penetration: $10M/yr
- 0.5% market penetration: $50M/yr
- 1.0% market penetration: $100M/yr
- 2.0% market penetration: $200M/yr
- Valuation: $1B - $2B

</v-clicks>

# External Validation — Talk to Humans

<v-clicks>

- Some case studies didn't use "traditional" validation
- But they were agency owners:
  1. Painful problem
  2. Initial users were clients
  3. Developers at hand
- They rode the wave

</v-clicks>

popularized by Eric Ries in 2011 with "The Lean Startup".

1. They were eating their own dog food. They were directly solving a problem that they had every single day and this usually looks like automating a manual process. # Why External Validation is Uncomfortable

<v-clicks>

- **Three common fears**:
  1. Not having something **tangible** to show
  2. Blowing our **one big opportunity** to sell
  3. **Rejection** and idea death
- Better to kill bad ideas **early and cheap**

</v-clicks>

<v-clicks>

- Wrong questions, wrong people

</v-clicks>

# Wrong

<v-clicks>

**You:** Mom, I have a business idea. Do you have 5 minutes?

**Mom:** Of course, dear.

*I'm proud of you, and I don't want to hurt your feelings.*

**You:** You like your iPad and use it a lot?

**Mom:** Sure, it's great.

*I use it to check email on the sofa.*

**You:** Would you buy a cookbook app?

**Mom:** I love cookbooks, sounds nice. Does it come with vegan recipes? Or something special for Xmas?

*Well, I have plenty of cookbooks. I don't need a computer in my kitchen – it might get dirty! But hey, if my kid made it, I'll try. App? I never bought an app. Don't you need to enter your credit card for that? Let me try to change the subject.*

</v-clicks>

---

# Correct

<v-clicks>

**You:** Mom, when have you last used the iPad? For what?

**Mom:** Last night, I was reading the news and checking emails on the couch.

**You:** Have you ever used it in the kitchen?

**Mom:** No, I'm worried it might get messy or damaged.

**You:** Have you ever bought an app? Which? Why? For how much?

**Mom:** I bought Solitaire for $2.99 because the ads were annoying me. That's the only one.

**You:** Do you use your cookbooks?

**Mom:** Not really, they're mostly decorative now. I usually just Google recipes.

**You:** Is there anything you dislike about them?

**Mom:** They take up space, and I can never find the recipe I want quickly.

**You:** What was the last cookbook you bought? When? Why?

**Mom:** About three years ago, it was a Jamie Oliver book because I saw it on sale and thought the photos looked nice.

</v-clicks>

# Personal Story: NextHaven.ai

<v-clicks>

- **Problem**: Time-consuming and stressful accommodation hunting for digital nomads
- **Solution**: AI matchmaker bringing best fit accommodations from top platforms
- **The Journey**: A case study in validation done wrong (and what we learned)

</v-clicks>

---

# Problem Discovery: When Friends Complain

<v-clicks>

- **Problem-mining moment**: Two friends started complaining about the same problem I was facing
- **The pain**: Settling for less-than-ideal accommodations due to stress and time constraints
- **Personal experience**: Always procrastinating because finding the perfect place was annoying

</v-clicks>

---

# Desk Research: Proximity to Problem

<v-clicks>

- **My network**: Closest friends were all self-employed digital nomads (including me)
- **Perfect proximity**: We were living the problem daily
- **Time investment**: 1-2 hours each month finding perfect spots
- **Financial impact**: $60/month in time (@$30/hr)

</v-clicks>

---

# Head on Fire Test: Stress Analysis

<v-clicks>

- **30-minute rant session** among friends confirmed hair-on-fire status
- **Week of stress** leading up to finding new accommodation
- **Daily anxiety** before current place ran out
- **Procrastination pattern**: Avoided the task because it was so annoying

</v-clicks>

---

# Current Solutions & Market Sizing

<v-clicks>

- **Existing options**: Booking platforms, agents, referrals
- **Bottom-up calculation**:
  - Price = $60 ÷ 5 = $12/mo = $144/yr
  - 40M digital nomads, 70% (28M) self-employed
  - **Revenue potential**: 28M × $144 × 5% = **$202M ARR**
- **Growth trend**: 2-3% per year

</v-clicks>
