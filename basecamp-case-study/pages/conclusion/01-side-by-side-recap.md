# Final Recap — What They Did vs What We'd Do

<div class="text-left max-w-6xl mx-auto">

## Side-by-Side Comparison

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-8 mt-8">

<div>

### What Basecamp Did ✅

<v-clicks>

* **Agency foundation**: Stable revenue, client relationships, team chemistry
* **Organic pain discovery**: Internal workflow friction → product opportunity  
* **Constraint-driven development**: 6 weeks, $4.5k, essential features only
* **Dogfooding validation**: Real projects, real feedback, immediate adoption
* **Audience-first launch**: Signal v. Noise blog as distribution platform
* **Revenue discipline**: Waited for crossover before cutting agency services
* **Culture distribution**: Transparent development, strong opinions, community building

</v-clicks>

</div>

<div>

### What We'd Do Differently 🚀

<v-clicks>

* **Systematic fit check**: Go/no-go framework, clear success metrics upfront
* **Structured problem mining**: Pain scoring (R×I×F), opportunity mapping
* **21-day validation sprint**: Faster test/learn cycles, pivot thresholds
* **MVP positioning framework**: Against category, emotional job-to-be-done
* **Modern GTM engine**: Multi-channel approach, growth loop optimization
* **Transition playbook**: Systematic phase-out, client preservation strategy
* **Content strategy**: Document everything, build multiple distribution channels

</v-clicks>

</div>

</div>

  <em class="text-lg">Basecamp succeeded through disciplined execution and organic growth. Our playbook would accelerate their timeline and reduce risk through systematic validation and modern growth tactics.</em>
